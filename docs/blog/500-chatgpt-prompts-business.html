Free Sample Prompts

Here are 5 proven prompts you can use right now:

1. Marketing: Value Proposition Builder You are a positioning strategist. I run a [your business type] targeting [target audience]. We help them [core benefit]. Apply the "Jobs to Be Done" framework to identify: 1) What job do customers hire us for? 2) What are the functional, emotional, and social dimensions? 3) How do competitors fail at this job? 4) What's our unique advantage? Output a one-paragraph value proposition and a one-liner tagline.
2. Content: Blog Post Outline Generator You are an SEO content strategist. Create a detailed outline for a blog post titled "[topic]". Target keyword: "[keyword]". Include: meta description (max 155 chars), H2 sections with 3-5 bullet points each, FAQ section with 3-5 questions, internal linking suggestions, and a strong CTA. Use the "skyscraper technique" — make this outline more comprehensive than any competing article.
3. Sales: Cold Email Personalization You are a sales development expert. Draft a personalized cold email for [prospect name], title "[job title]" at [company]. Reference: [specific trigger event or company news]. Use the "AIDA framework": Attention (opening hook referencing trigger), Interest (relevant insight), Desire (specific value proposition), Action (low-friction CTA). Max 150 words.
4. Operations: Process Optimization You are a business operations consultant. Analyze the following process for bottlenecks: [describe process]. Apply the "Theory of Constraints" to: 1) Identify the current bottleneck, 2) Exploit the bottleneck (maximize throughput), 3) Subordinate everything else, 4) Elevate the bottleneck, 5) Repeat. Provide specific, actionable recommendations.
5. Customer Success: Churn Prevention You are a customer success manager. Draft a proactive outreach email to a customer showing these churn signals: [list signals: low engagement, feature non-usage, support tickets decreasing, etc.]. Use the "BEFORE framework": Believe (we noticed), Empathize (we understand), Find (let's find the gap), Offer (specific help), Re-engage (small win). Keep tone consultative, not salesy.
