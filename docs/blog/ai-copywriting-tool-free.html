Tips for Better AI Copy

Pro tip: The quality of AI copy depends on your prompt. Instead of "write sales copy," try: "Write a homepage hero section for a project management tool aimed at remote teams. Benefits: Slack integration, auto-timezone, async updates. Tone: professional but approachable."

Other prompt strategies that work:

  • Specify format: "Write this as a bullet list" or "as a 3-paragraph email."
  • Define audience: "For busy moms who meal prep" is better than "for users."
  • Include examples: "Like Apple's product page tone but for a plumbing service."
  • Iterate: Generate 3-5 variations, pick the best parts, remix them.

When Free AI Copywriting Is Enough

Free AI copy tools aren't right for every situation. Here's when they work — and when they don't:

Good for: First drafts, brainstorming, overcoming blank page syndrome, short-form copy (headlines, descriptions, CTAs), routine emails, social posts.

Not ideal for: Long-form sales pages requiring deep brand voice consistency, highly technical B2B copy, legal or medical copy, final-publish-quality without editing.

The smart workflow: use free AI tools to generate raw material fast, then layer in your human editing for polish, accuracy, and brand voice.
